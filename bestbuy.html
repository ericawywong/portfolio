Best Buy

Best Buy is one of the largest and fastest growing retail companies in North America.

The eNewsletter has become one of the major online promotion mediums that help retailers to increase sales. The existing Best Buy eNewsletter conversion rate had decreased due to the changes in technology and viewers’ behavior. I was tasked to redesign this piece to increase the conversion rate and direct viewers back to BestBuy.ca

My role

As the sole UI designer on the Digital Production team, I coordinated with the marketing manager and creative lead to create promotion materials such as emails, web banners, and motion graphics.

  • Concepting
  • Visual Design
  • Production

The Problems

  • Branding not up to date
  • Everything is bold, viewers can easily lose focus
  • Too many products and copies
  • Not eligible on mobile devices due to spacing and layout
  • Not mobile responsive

The Challanges

One of the core challenges was convincing the marketing team to reduce the copy and content on the eNewsletter.

The idea was to keep the eNewsletter clean and allow viewers’ to see the product and its description within 1-2 seconds. Yet, from the marketing perspective, this may negatively affect sales in different product categories. I began to wonder if viewers would mind scrolling through a long eNewsletter, so we did a test.

Old

New

The Changes

  • Style update
  • Eliminate unnecessary copy and restrict number of characters
  • Enlarge product images so the product can “speak” for itself
  • Enlarge clickable area for other promotional banners and stories. Make it easier to click on when on mobile devices.
  • Due to our budget, we were unable to build a mobile version or have the email responsive. So I reduced the width of the eNewsletter and created the products 2 across instead of 4 for better mobile experience.

The result

The new version has increased the conversion rate by 17%.

Other Benefits

Aside from increasing the conversion rate, I also built various templates to speed up the design process. I collaborated with the marketing manager to establish an in-house process to reduce production time and cost.
