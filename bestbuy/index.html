Case Study: Best Buy eNewsletters Redesign

At Best Buy, I worked as a solo designer on the digital newsletters team, contributing to UI, production, and coordination efforts. As part of a cross-functional team including art directors, merchandisers, and marketers, I identified an opportunity to modernize a legacy email format that no longer served users in a mobile-first world.

üîç Challenge: Desktop-Centric Design in a Mobile Era

The existing newsletter layout, originally built for desktop viewing, struggled on mobile screens. Common issues included:

  • Dense blocks of copy
  • Small, low-impact images
  • Overcrowded layouts with minimal visual breathing room
  • Poor mobile usability and touch interactions

These problems led to low engagement rates and a suboptimal user experience, highlighting the need for a responsive redesign.

üí° Insight: Design for Mobile-First Behavior

Data and stakeholder feedback confirmed that most users were accessing newsletters on mobile. We also saw that users skimmed content quickly, focused on visuals, and interacted primarily through taps.

  • Mobile opens accounted for over 60% of total email views
  • Users spent an average of 8 seconds on the email before clicking through or closing
  • Click-through rates were highest on visually engaging elements like large images and buttons

Solution: A Single, Adaptive Layout Built for Engagement

I led the design of a responsive newsletter system that works seamlessly on both desktop and mobile:

  • Simplified layout for faster scanning
  • Visual consistency with the Best Buy e-commerce site
  • Reduced copy, highlighting only key messaging
  • Motion graphics added to increase interaction
  • Enlarged imagery & CTAs for mobile users
  • One adaptive version (vs. multiple builds), saving both time and cost

Validation: Real-World A/B Testing

To test the impact, we ran A/B tests in collaboration with a third-party agency. Two email versions (old vs. new) were sent to randomly selected, equal-sized Canadian subscriber segments to measure performance differences.
