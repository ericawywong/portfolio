Case Study:
Streamlining Design Handoff for E-Newsletters

When I joined the newly formed team as the sole designer, I adopted the existing workflow after consulting with the project manager, creative leadership, marketing, and merchandisers. It was the process everyone was familiar with.

However, as our workload increased, cracks in the system began to show—especially around misaligned communication, inconsistent feedback, and repeated last-minute revisions.

🔍 Challenge: Fragmented Feedback & Rising Costs

What began as a straightforward process—merchandisers submitting content two weeks before launch—became disorganized and reactive. Designers were inundated with edit requests via email, chat, and ad-hoc calls, often overlapping with final development.

As the team scaled, the issues compounded:

  • 🕰️ Overtime hours

    Designers working late to accommodate sudden revisions

  • 😖 Burnout & stress

    Unpredictable workloads and communication breakdowns

  • 💸 Increased costs

    Frequent revision rounds submitted to the outsourcing agency

💡 Insight: Design Needs Structure to Scale

Observing the chaos, I realized that without centralized communication and enforced deadlines, the design process would remain reactive and expensive. The root issue wasn’t creativity—it was the lack of structure around it.

With multiple departments involved—project managers, merchandisers, copywriters, developers, and designers—we needed a shared framework that enforced consistency, accountability, and clarity.

Solution: A 2-Week Sprint & Online Proofing

I introduced a structured two-week process with two fixed edit windows, supported by an online proofing tool to house all feedback in one place.

Week 1: Review & Mockup

  • Merchandisers submit content (product info, pricing, copy)
  • Designers create mockups
  • Stakeholders give feedback in one scheduled, in-person meeting

Week 2: Finalization

  • Only typo-level edits permitted
  • Mockups sent for localization
  • inal assets handed off to developers

Any feedback outside the proofing tool was no longer accepted—this maintained control and ensured accountability.

Overcoming Implementation Hurdles

Aligning Cross-Functional Teams

With so many hands involved, standardizing communication took deliberate effort. We worked across departments to ensure buy-in.

Navigating Change Resistance

Stakeholders were used to the old way of working. To smooth the transition:

  • We tracked the source and timing of edits
  • Held retrospectives every two e-newsletters
  • Identified unnecessary revisions and removed them from future cycles

🌟 Impact: A Smarter, Calmer Design Workflow

  • ✅ Production costs significantly reduced
  • ⏱️ Faster design-to-dev turnaround time
  • 📉 Last-minute changes dropped dramatically
  • 🗂️ All feedback captured in one place—no more missed edits
  • 😊 A healthier, more sustainable working environment for the team

Why This Matters

This case study reflects how UX thinking can extend beyond screens—into process design, collaboration, and team culture. It showcases leadership, operational problem-solving, and an ability to scale creative work through structure.
