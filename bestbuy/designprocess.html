The original design process

It was introduced to me as a "simple" process where the merchandisers will submit product info and copies to us 2 weeks priror to launch.

In the "everything is prefect" scenioro, we would simply plug in the content and send it off to localization and our outsourcing agent for implemenentation.

Things didn't go as planned. We begin to expereice spotonaious and large amount of edits scattered in different emails, text, calls and time frames.

With the team expanding, the emphasis on streamlined processes became increasingly crucial. I observed with the original design process...

Endless last-minute edits

Stressful envoirment

Additional costs due to late changes

Refined design process

I proposed a two-week process featuring only two deadlines.

In the initial week, inventory merchandisers submitted their products, copies, and prices to designers for mockups. Stakeholders will provide their edits during an in-person review meeting.

A final round of review will be conducted online, and copies will be sent for localization. Subsequently, all essential assets will be forwarded to the outsourcing team for development.

An online proofing tool was introduced to keep all comments in one place. Any edits submitted outside of the tool was prohibited.

The challanges

Connect with everyone

The number of memebers that are involved in creating one e-newsletter was surprisingly large, including: the project mangers, product marchandisers for each of the categories, the art directors, the copywriters and the designers.

Retrospacs

When this was first introduced, we have a lot of set backs where a lot of the stakeholders were too used to the original process and having a hard time adjusting.

In order to reinforcsed this process, when there are a last min request, we will keep track of all the edits and have a retrospec meeting every 2 cycles.

This process was exteremly impactful as it lays out the reasons for the edits and evulate if they are neccessary and essentally elimates the unnesscary ones.

Results

  • Successfully reduced production costs
  • Enhanced overall team efficiency
  • Last min edits, unforuntualy, are still unavoidable (since we are only human); however, the number of these edits have drastically decreased.
  • Edits and comments are kept in one place; therefore, elimated overlooked requests.
